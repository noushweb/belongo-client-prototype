Privacy Policy

Last updated 1 June 2026 · effective immediately

This policy explains what we collect, why, and the control you have over it. We’ve written it in plain language first — the detail is below if you want it.

✦ In plain English
  • ✓We collect only what a booking needs — your name, contact, payment token and trip details.
  • ✓We never sell your data. Card numbers are held by Stripe, never on our servers.
  • ✓You can download or delete everything from Account settings anytime.
  • ✓AI reads reviews to help guests — it doesn’t profile you or make decisions about you.

1 What we collect

We collect the minimum needed to find you a stay, take payment and keep your booking safe:

  • Account & contact: name, email, mobile, and your preferences (currency, language, prayer-aware browsing).
  • Booking data: stays you view and book, dates, guest counts and messages with hosts.
  • Payment: a secure token from our processor — we never see or store your full card number.
  • Device & usage: basic analytics to keep the site fast and fix problems.
  • Community contributions: reviews, photos and “Nearby, by guests” places you add (shown with your first name).

2 How we use it

Your data does three jobs, and only these: it completes your bookings, it keeps your account and payments secure, and it improves what we show you (with your consent).

We send booking-critical messages — confirmations, payment reminders, check-in codes — because they keep your stay safe; these can’t be switched off. Everything else (deals, community replies) you control in Account settings.

3 Sharing & processors

We do not sell your personal data. We share only what each partner needs to do their job:

  • Hosts & property managers: your name and trip details, so they can host you.
  • Payment processor (Stripe): to take and refund payments securely.
  • Communications (email/SMS): to deliver confirmations and reminders.
  • Authorities: only where the law requires it.

4 Payments & security

Card details are tokenised and held by our PCI-compliant processor — they never touch Belongo servers. Connections are encrypted in transit. We support local methods (mada, Apple Pay) precisely because they keep cross-border payments both reliable and secure.

You can enable two-factor authentication anytime from Account settings → Security.

5 AI & recommendations

Our AI assistant reads public, verified-stay reviews to summarise what guests say — about noise, cleanliness, the walk to the mosque. It works on the property’s content to help you decide; it does not build a profile of you or make automated decisions about your account.

Personalised recommendations use your activity only with your consent, which you can withdraw under Privacy & data.

6 Your rights

Wherever you are, you can access, correct, export or delete your data. In one place:

  • Download my data — a full copy of your bookings, reviews and profile.
  • Delete account — permanently removes your data (active bookings must be settled first).
  • Withdraw consent — turn off personalisation or marketing anytime.

All available from Account settings, or by emailing privacy@belongo.com.

7 Retention

We keep booking and payment records for as long as the law requires (typically for tax and dispute windows), then delete or anonymise them. Reviews and community contributions remain to help future guests, shown only with your first name — you can remove them anytime.

8 Contact

Questions about your privacy, or want to exercise a right? Our data protection team replies within 30 days, usually much sooner.
